**Maharashtrian Dishes

1.VEG

1.batata Vada

Batata Vada is a popular vegetarian fast food dish from the Indian state of Maharashtra.
It is a mashed potato patty coated with chickpea flour, which is then deep-fried and served hot with chutney.

1.Aamras

Aamras is a popular dessert from the Western Indian states of Maharashtra and Gujarat. It is made by blending ripe and juicy mangoes into a creamy, sweet, and fragrant mixture. The finely puréed cream usually gains the sweetness from ripe mangos, but it can be additionally sweetened with jaggery or sugar. It is sometimes merely elevated with the addition of saffron, dry ginger, or cardamom, but its simplicity allows room for alteration and the creation of numerous varieties

3.bharli Vangi

Bharli Vangi is a popular Maharashtrian dish that consists of small eggplants stuffed with a spicy masala and slow-cooked in a gravy base of coconut, sesame seeds, peanuts, onions, tomatoes, and spices The stuffing is usually made with a combination of garlic, ginger, tomatoes, roasted peanuts, jaggery, turmeric, and red chili powder 3. The dish is typically served with rice or roti

4.Bhelpuri

Bhel Puri is a popular Indian street food snack that originated in Mumbai, Maharashtra. It is a savory chaat made of puffed rice, vegetables, and a tangy tamarind sauce, and has a crunchy texture 1. The dish is typically served as a beach snack or comfort food.

5.modak

Modak is a sweet dumpling that is believed to have originated in the state of Maharashtra, India 1. It is a popular dessert that is typically made during the Hindu festival of Ganesh Chaturthi 2. The dumpling is made from rice flour and is filled with a sweet mixture of grated coconut and jaggery
. The filling can also be flavored with cardamom or nutmeg

6.Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish that originated in Mumbai, Maharashtra.
It is a spicy, tangy and fiery mashed mixed vegetable curry topped with fresh zesty coriander, sharp red onions and a slab of butter.

The dish is made out of a very flavorful thick vegetable curry (bhaji) eaten with a soft bread roll (pav)

7.Pithla

Pithla is a traditional Maharashtrian dish made using chickpea flour or besan.
It is a stew-like curry that is cooked along with garlic, green chilies, curry leaves, onions, coriander leaves, and spices 1.
It is usually served with Bhakri or rice for a hearty meal that is also gluten-free and vegan 2

8.Puran Poli

Puran Poli is a traditional Maharashtrian sweet flatbread that is typically made during festivals, family gatherings, and auspicious occasions 1.
It is a delectable combination of a sweet lentil filling called “puran” encased in a soft and thin wheat flour dough

Sabudana Khichdi, also known as Sabudana Usal, is a popular dish in Maharashtra1.
It’s often made during Hindu fasting seasons like Mahashivratri, Navratri, and Shravan1. Not only is it a fasting food, but it’s also one of the popular street foods in Maharashtra and is available throughout the year.

9.Shrikhand

Shrikhand is a traditional sweet from Maharashtra1.
It is prepared from ‘hung curd’, known as ‘chakka’ in Marathi1.
The dish is often flavored with keshar (saffron) and pistachios1.
It is enjoyed during festivals with hot puffed puris1. Shrikhand is a thick, creamy, and delicious Indian dessert made with strained yogurt, powdered sugar, cardamoms, and sometimes saffron & nuts2

10.Vada Pav

Vada Pav, alternatively spelled as Wada Pav, is a popular dish native to Maharashtra1.
It’s often referred to as the “Indian burger” and is a part of the culture of Maharashtra1.
The dish consists of a deep-fried potato dumpling placed inside a bread bun (pav) sliced almost in half through the middle1.
It is generally accompanied with one or more chutneys and a fried green chili1

11.Poha for breakefast

Poha, also known as Kanda Poha, is a very popular breakfast or tea time snack in Maharashtra1.
It is made from flattened or beaten rice and tossed in caramelized onions, green chillies, and peanuts1.
If you prefer to eat a light meal first thing in the morning, this super quick dish is just right for you

1.Handi Chicken

Handi Chicken is a popular dish in Maharashtra1.
It is a Maharashtrian-style chicken cooked in a Handi.
A Handi is an earthen pot, and when you make food in earthen pots, it retains oil and gives moisture to the food1.
The usual Maharashtrian curry has a lot of oil in it, and adding a handi to it increases its nutritional value too1.
